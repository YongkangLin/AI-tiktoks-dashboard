# Microdrama Plot Bank

Use this as a reusable plot engine library for Fruit Crush Villa and future AI drama themes. Do not copy named shows, characters, episode sequences, or dialogue. Reuse the mechanics: hidden identity, public reversal, forced relationship, revenge setup, second chance, betrayal proof, and cliffhanger payoff.

## Research Notes

- Screenburn describes microdrama as its own vertical-screen format, not a compressed TV episode. Each episode should run on hook, escalation, and cliffhanger.
  Source: https://screenburn.app/learn/microdrama-structure
- RADII's survey of Chinese microdramas highlights flash marriages, secret CEOs, revenge fantasies, taboo romances, reincarnation/time travel, and low-friction melodrama as dominant tropes.
  Source: https://radii.co/article/10-binge-worthy-chinese-microdramas
- ThisReels describes short drama as 60-120 second vertical episodes with cliffhangers every episode, high plot density, and common buckets like rebirth/second chance.
  Source: https://thisreels.com/blog/what-is-short-drama
- Vitrina frames microdrama as serialized 60-90 second mobile-first storytelling where the cliffhanger resolves in the next episode's opening and the next hook lands before the end.
  Source: https://vitrina.ai/blog/micro-dramas-and-vertical-first-storytelling/
- Yale Journal of International Affairs notes that cliffhangers function as the conversion prompt in duanju, making "what happens next" the product.
  Source: https://www.yalejournal.org/publications/micro-drama-as-soft-power-yedbr

## Current Plot Clarity Verdict

The previous `The Sour Receipt` and `The Card Was Already Signed` versions were usable, but they were too dense for Episode 1.

The viewer has to track five ideas:

1. Marco entered early.
2. Stella may already know him.
3. Bella has timestamps.
4. Benny may have staged a test on Stella.

For microdrama, the better Episode 1 version should make the emotional question obvious:

> Did Marco lie about knowing Stella, and why does Benny have hidden footage from last night?

Everything else should support that one question.

## Simplification Rule

For first episodes, use only three proof objects:

1. One physical proof object.
2. One behavior clue.
3. One final reversal object.

The current first-render script, `The Necklace He Denied`, follows this rule:

- Physical proof: Stella and Marco have matching seed-heart necklaces.
- Behavior clue: Marco touches his own necklace while pretending not to know Stella.
- Final reversal object: Marco's necklace engraving says `S + M - 11:47 PM`, and Benny accidentally reveals there is an audition tape.

The removed receipt, consent-card, mango-pulp, and rehearsal-day clues can come later if the audience understands the core Stella/Marco/Benny betrayal first.

## Reusable Plot Engines

### 1. Secret Identity Romance

**Human trope:** poor stranger is secretly CEO or heir.

**Fruit version:** a new fruit arrives pretending to be a normal contestant, but they secretly own the villa or designed the game.

**Episode 1 shape:**

- Hook: someone insults the "new fruit."
- Turn: the host quietly obeys the new fruit.
- Cliffhanger: the villa contract has the new fruit's signature.

### 2. Flash Marriage / Forced Couple

**Human trope:** strangers accidentally or contractually marry, then one is secretly rich or powerful.

**Fruit version:** two fruits are forced to pair for the first challenge, but the pairing card was signed before they met.

**Episode 1 shape:**

- Hook: "Why does this card say we chose each other yesterday?"
- Turn: one fruit knows the other's private preference.
- Cliffhanger: the host says, "This couple was selected before casting."

### 3. Revenge Return

**Human trope:** wronged protagonist returns richer, prettier, or more powerful.

**Fruit version:** a fruit who was juiced out of the villa returns as a premium contestant with immunity.

**Episode 1 shape:**

- Hook: the eliminated fruit walks back in.
- Turn: the villain's alliance realizes the fruit cannot be voted out.
- Cliffhanger: the returnee plays a recording from the night they were betrayed.

### 4. Second Chance / Rebirth

**Human trope:** protagonist wakes up before the betrayal and uses future knowledge.

**Fruit version:** Stella gets a "redo card" after losing the first vote and remembers every betrayal from the previous timeline.

**Episode 1 shape:**

- Hook: Stella says the exact sentence another fruit has not said yet.
- Turn: she prevents the first setup.
- Cliffhanger: someone else remembers too.

### 5. Hidden Baby / Secret Family

**Human trope:** child reveals hidden relationship or inheritance.

**Fruit version:** a tiny pineapple appears with a bracelet matching two contestants, implying a secret villa history.

**Episode 1 shape:**

- Hook: a baby fruit calls the wrong contestant "dad."
- Turn: the bracelet proves the baby came from the villa's locked room.
- Cliffhanger: the host says, "That baby was born during Season Zero."

### 6. Public Humiliation Reversal

**Human trope:** villain humiliates protagonist, then proof flips the room.

**Fruit version:** a fruit is mocked for being "unripe," but the taste-test judges pick them first.

**Episode 1 shape:**

- Hook: someone dumps a smoothie on the underdog.
- Turn: blind taste-test reveals the underdog made the winning drink.
- Cliffhanger: the villain's cup has sabotage powder.

### 7. Contract Trap

**Human trope:** protagonist signs a contract without knowing the real clause.

**Fruit version:** every contestant signs a villa rule card, but one clause secretly gives the winner control over couples.

**Episode 1 shape:**

- Hook: "You signed away your first choice."
- Turn: the contestant realizes someone swapped their card.
- Cliffhanger: the swapper is their partner.

### 8. Substitute Bride / Mistaken Identity

**Human trope:** one sister replaces another in a marriage arrangement.

**Fruit version:** one fruit enters under another fruit's casting profile.

**Episode 1 shape:**

- Hook: the host announces the wrong name.
- Turn: the fake contestant knows the real contestant's secrets.
- Cliffhanger: the real contestant arrives at the villa gate.

### 9. Forbidden Pairing

**Human trope:** romance is forbidden by family, class, contract, or revenge.

**Fruit version:** two fruits are forbidden to pair because their juice blends are "incompatible," but the chemistry score says they are perfect.

**Episode 1 shape:**

- Hook: the compatibility screen flashes 100 percent, then the host cuts power.
- Turn: another fruit says the same result happened last season.
- Cliffhanger: the deleted result reappears on every phone.

### 10. Fake Weakness

**Human trope:** protagonist pretends to be powerless to expose betrayal.

**Fruit version:** a fruit pretends to be naive and sweet while secretly testing who lies under pressure.

**Episode 1 shape:**

- Hook: the sweet fruit catches a lie but smiles.
- Turn: everyone underestimates them and confesses too much.
- Cliffhanger: the sweet fruit has been wearing a hidden mic.

## Best First-Render Choice

For the first paid test, use either:

1. **Secret Identity Romance**, because it is immediately legible and maps cleanly to the Fruit Crush Villa host/test mechanic.
2. **Public Humiliation Reversal**, because it is visually obvious and emotionally simple.
3. **Flash Marriage / Forced Couple**, because it creates instant romance stakes without needing much backstory.

`The Sour Receipt` has been simplified into `The Necklace He Denied`, using **Public Humiliation Reversal + Betrayal Proof**:

- Marco publicly denies knowing Stella.
- Stella's matching necklace is the first proof.
- Bella reading Marco's timestamp is the final proof.
- Benny's "audition tape" slip creates the Part 2 promise.

## Prompt Rule For Future Scripts

When generating a new script, ask for:

```text
Use a proven microdrama plot engine, but make it original to humanoid fruit/object characters.
Keep Episode 1 simple enough to explain in one sentence.
Use one physical proof object, one behavior clue, and one final reversal.
Do not introduce more than four named characters in the first 60 seconds.
End with a specific question that Episode 2 answers immediately.
```
