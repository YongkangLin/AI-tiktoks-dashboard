# Monetization Workflow

This project is designed for original AI story series, not reposted clips or imitation accounts. Revenue eligibility depends on the platform, country, account status, and current rules.

## Current TikTok-Oriented Guardrails

As of May 30, 2026, TikTok support materials for Creator Rewards emphasize:

- Original, high-quality content.
- Videos at least one minute long.
- Personal accounts in eligible regions and in good standing.
- Eligible videos beginning reward collection only after reaching the required qualified-view threshold.
- Compliance with Community Guidelines, Terms of Service, and Copyright Policy.
- No artificial view inflation, paid-view manipulation, or automated engagement.
- AI-generated content is not treated as automatically prohibited, but realistic AI-generated or significantly edited content needs clear disclosure and must not mislead, impersonate, or violate other guidelines.

## Monetization-Aware Creative Rules

- Make the show name original. Do not use protected TV franchise names in titles, captions, usernames, or hashtags.
- Keep runtime above 61 seconds when targeting one-minute reward programs.
- Generate fresh scenes for each episode instead of reusing the same clip with changed captions.
- Use recurring characters, but vary conflicts, locations, and reveals.
- Design for retention: hook in 0 to 3 seconds, reveal the core conflict by 10 seconds, introduce a choice by 35 seconds, and end with an unresolved decision.
- Track series performance by episode number, hook type, runtime, average watch time, completion rate, comments, and follows.
- Review the generated `monetization_audit.md` before producing assets or publishing.

## Revenue Paths To Layer Later

- Creator rewards for eligible original long-form short videos.
- Series compilation videos on YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels, and Facebook Reels.
- Affiliate links only when the fictional story does not mislead viewers.
- Character merch after repeated audience demand.
- Sponsored episodes only with clear disclosure and platform-compliant branded content handling.

## Weekly Operating Cadence

- Monday: generate 20 episode packages and select the top 10 hooks.
- Tuesday to Thursday: produce and edit 2 to 4 finished episodes per day.
- Friday: review analytics, identify strongest characters and conflict types, and rewrite templates.
- Weekend: publish compiled recaps, polls, and follow-up episodes based on comments.
